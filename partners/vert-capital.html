Vert Capital's technical architecture enables the tokenization of Brazilian securitizations on XRPL while maintaining full regulatory compliance with CVM requirements and Brazilian capital markets infrastructure.

Vert Capital works with originators (banks, fintechs, corporates) to structure securitizations per CVM requirements. Legal documentation finalized. SPV (Special Purpose Vehicle) established. Credit ratings obtained from Brazilian rating agencies.

2

3

Investor Onboarding

Investors complete CVM-compliant KYC/AML procedures. Accreditation verified per investor category. Whitelisted wallets enabled. Brazilian investors require valid CPF; foreign investors require equivalent documentation.

4

Primary Subscription

Investors subscribe through platform with BRL settled via traditional banking. Tokens minted on XRPL upon subscription confirmation. Fractional ownership enables BRL 1,000 minimum investments. Investor registry maintained.

5

Cash Flow Distribution

Underlying securitization generates principal and interest payments. Distribution events triggered by off-chain servicer confirmation. Payments distributed pro-rata to token holders. Full transparency of payment calculations.

6

Secondary Trading

Token holders can trade positions on XRPL DEX. Price discovery reflects credit risk and market conditions. Settlement in 4 seconds eliminates settlement risk. Trading restricted to verified eligible investors.

Risk Factors & Mitigation Strategies

Currency & FX Risk
Risk: BRL volatility against USD could significantly impact returns for international investors.

Mitigation: FX hedging products available. Natural hedge through BRL-denominated cash flows. Option to convert returns at maturity.
Credit Risk
Risk: Default by underlying borrowers could impact securitization cash flows and investor returns.

Mitigation: Diversification across multiple borrowers and sectors. Credit enhancement structures (overcollateralization, subordination). Independent rating agency assessment.
Regulatory Risk
Risk: Changes in CVM regulations or Brazilian capital markets rules could impact operations.

Mitigation: Active regulatory engagement. Flexible architecture to adapt. Jurisdictional diversification where possible.
Interest Rate Risk
Risk: Brazilian interest rate (SELIC) changes impact floating-rate returns and fixed-rate valuations.

Mitigation: Mix of fixed and floating rate structures. Duration management. Market price reflects rate expectations.
Liquidity Risk
Risk: Secondary market may have limited depth, especially for larger positions.

Mitigation: Market maker participation. Issuer repurchase commitments. XRPL DEX provides continuous trading opportunity.
Operational Risk
Risk: Technology failures or servicer issues could disrupt cash flow processing.

Mitigation: Multiple servicer backup. Robust technology infrastructure. Disaster recovery procedures.
