üá∏üá¨ Singapore MAS Framework

Singapore's Monetary Authority (MAS) has established one of the world's most comprehensive regulatory frameworks for digital payment token (DPT) services, providing clarity and investor protection while fostering innovation. Under the Payment Services Act (PSA), digital asset service providers must obtain appropriate licenses, maintain segregated client assets, implement robust KYC/AML controls, and comply with technology risk management guidelines. Phillip Securities operates under full MAS supervision, ensuring compliance with all applicable regulations.

The MAS regulatory framework includes specific provisions for tokenized securities, distinguishing between digital payment tokens (which have tighter restrictions) and digital securities (which can be offered under existing securities laws). This nuanced approach enables Phillip Securities to offer tokenized money market products to accredited and retail investors while maintaining appropriate investor protections. The regulatory clarity has attracted major global institutions to establish digital asset operations in Singapore.

  • PSA licensing
  • Segregated client assets
  • KYC/AML compliance
  • Technology risk guidelines
  • Securities vs DPT distinction

‚öôÔ∏è Crossing Engine Mechanics

Phillip Securities operates a sophisticated internal crossing engine that matches buy and sell orders for tokenized assets within its platform, providing enhanced liquidity and price improvement for investors. The crossing engine aggregates liquidity from all POEMS users, enabling execution at better prices than would be available through external market makers alone. This internal liquidity pool is particularly valuable for Asian trading sessions when global markets may be closed.

The crossing engine operates with strict price-time priority, ensuring fair and transparent execution for all participants. For larger orders, the system can route to external liquidity providers to ensure execution at optimal prices. The engine also supports algorithmic trading strategies, enabling institutional investors to implement sophisticated execution algorithms while maintaining compliance with regulatory requirements.

Platform RiskLow

Counterparty RiskMedium
